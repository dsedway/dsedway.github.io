The History of Basketball Shoes

Basketball has been around for around 130 years. Like other sports, special equipment was necessary when playing the sport. Similar to football and hockey, a shoe unique to the sport had to be made

Original Converse

The first basketball shoes were converse, made in the 1920s. A crucial component of basketball shoes is traction, and basketball shoes need to have good traction. Notice how the poster advertises "non-skid." This shoe laid the foundation for the rest of basketball shoes.

Nike Blazer Mid 77

Now frequently worn as everyday shoes, the OG Nike Blazer Mids were originally used on the hardwood. These shoes started a long run of legendary Nike basketball shoes that would eventually become part of mainstream culture and lifestyle.

Nike Jordan 1

Unparalleled in popularity amongst sneakerheads and hoopers alike, the Jordan 1 is arguably the most popular basketball shoe of all time. Now worn to look good, Michael Jordan debuted his first NBA game in these shoes.

Adidas TMAC 3

Tracy McGrady's 3rd signature sneaker with Adidas might not look pleasing, but these sneakers are beloved in the basketball community. Around the 2000s is when basketball shoes began to look less like lifestyle shoes and more geared toward basketball.

Nike Kobe 5

Some say it's the greatest basketball shoe of all time. The Kobe 5 is part of a group of incredibly stylish and capable basketball shoes from the early 2010s that are timeless. It is one of the most frequently-worn basketball shoes in the NBA.
